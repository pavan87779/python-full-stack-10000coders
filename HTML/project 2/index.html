The two-part cinematic saga, beginning with Kalki 2898 AD (often referred to as Part 1), blends Hindu mythology with dystopian science fiction. The story is set in the year 2898 AD, in a shattered, resource-depleted Earth where the last city of Kasi is ruled by the totalitarian god-king Supreme Yaskin from a technologically advanced, inverted pyramid called The Complex. The film establishes that Ashwatthama, an immortal warrior from the Mahabharata, has been wandering for 6,000 years, cursed to await and protect the arrival of Kalki, the final avatar of Vishnu, as an act of penance. The central conflict revolves around SUM-80 (Sumathi), a pregnant woman from the impoverished lower city, who is crucial to Yaskin's experiments (Project K) to harvest a life-extending serum. She escapes the Complex and is sought by the bounty hunter Bhairava, who is motivated by the reward units that would grant him entry into the privileged Complex. The pursuit is complicated by Ashwatthama, who attempts to protect Sumathi as the prophesied mother of Kalki. In the climactic battle of the first film, Bhairava unknowingly uses an ancient artifact, revealing him to be the reincarnation of the legendary warrior Karna. After defeating a major antagonist and protecting Sumathi, the film ends with Bhairava joining the rebels and Ashwatthama, while Yaskin injects himself with a sample of Sumathi's fetal serum and gains immense power, including the ability to wield Arjuna's divine bow, the Gandiva, setting the stage for the second part.

About Director



Nag Ashwin is recognized as one of the most ambitious and visionary directors in contemporary Indian cinema. After gaining critical acclaim with the National Award-winning biopic Mahanati (2018), he took on the mammoth task of writing and directing the epic science-fiction film Kalki 2898 AD. His vision for this project was to blend Indian mythology, particularly elements from the Mahabharata and the concept of the final Avatar of Vishnu (Kalki), with a sprawling, futuristic dystopian world set in 2898 AD.

Music Director



The music for Kalki 2898 AD was composed by Santhosh Narayanan, a highly celebrated music director predominantly known for his innovative and genre-blending work in Tamil cinema. His score for this epic science-fiction film, which marked his 50th film as a composer, was a crucial element in building the dystopian world envisioned by director Nag Ashwin.
